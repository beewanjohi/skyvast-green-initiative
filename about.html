Who we are

The bridge between global capital and Africa's drylands

SkyVast Green Initiative (SGI) is a U.S.-registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit transforming Kenya's arid and semi-arid lands (ASALs) through water-smart, community-powered solutions that are local, data-driven and deeply rooted in equity.

Conceived after an encounter in Diani, Kwale County in 2017 and formally registered as a U.S. 501(c)(3) in 2021, we operate from two homes — Acworth, Georgia in the United States and Thindigua, Kenya — uniting ten members across two continents to offer donors both tax-deductible giving and direct, visible impact on the ground. Today we work across nine counties, instrumenting every project with technology so resilience is something you can measure, not just hope for.

Our conviction

It began with two girls and a dried-up riverbed.

SkyVast Green Initiative was born in 2017 in Diani, Kwale County, after we met two girls — neither older than nine — bent under jerry cans nearly their own size, having walked over ten kilometres to draw water from a dried-up riverbed.

Shaken by the encounter, we drove them home and resolved to respond. Back in the United States, our founder gathered like-minded partners and, in 2021, formally registered SkyVast Green Initiative as a U.S. 501(c)(3). Today the organisation unites ten members — five in the diaspora and five in Kenya — working in tandem across continents to alleviate drought and engineer lasting resilience across Kenya's Arid and Semi-Arid Lands.

That first conviction still governs every decision: no community should be defined by the distance to its nearest water. We don't hand out relief — we engineer self-reliant, women- and youth-led economies that outlast us.
